<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The bomber jacket is (back) in. Formerly named the </span><a href="http://www.esquire.com/style/mens-fashion/a34543/history-flight-jackets/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">MA-1</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, it was originally designed for </span><a href="http://www.armysurplusworld.com/display.asp?subDepartmentID=277" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">military pilots</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> back in the day. This outerwear piece has gone from a bulky, </span><a href="http://www.chemheritage.org/discover/media/magazine/articles/26-3-nylon-a-revolution-in-textiles.aspx" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">nylon</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> jacket, to a tailored top layer. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A jacket that's available in more variation than that sold in your local </span><a href="http://www.armynavydeals.com/asp/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Army/Navy surplus store</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, it is now made with different fabrics, styles and tailoring. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">It can be styled to look somewhat </span><a href="http://hollywoodlife.com/2015/10/01/kendall-jenner-jacket-balmain-paris-fashion-week-outfit-pics/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">sporty</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://glamradar.com/how-to-look-chic-in-bomber-jackets/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">glammed up</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> or </span><a href="http://www.witwhimsy.com/2016/02/09/the-bomber-jacket/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">casually chic</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. To help you sift through this all, we’re here to give you pointers on how to style it, what silhouettes work for you and what to expect.  </span></p>
<p><b>Layering</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> -- From East to West Coast, the bomber jacket can adjust to nearly any region’s climates. To weather the various temperatures, make the </span><a href="http://www.wikihow.com/Layer-Clothes" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">layering technique</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> your best friend. Bomber jackets nowadays possess a more feminine tailoring, which will eliminate any bulkiness when layering sweaters and thicker knit garments.</span></p>
<p><b>Fabric</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> -- Not all bomber jackets are created equal. Consider different fabrics for different looks and occasions. For </span><a href="http://www.harpersbazaar.com/winter-fashion/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">autumn/winter</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, try wearing a bomber jacket made of suede, leather or a soft wool, like Japanese wool. When </span><a href="http://cupofjo.com/2016/02/12-spring-2016-fashion-trends/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">spring</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> rolls in, try searching for lighter fabric, like </span><a href="https://www.fabric.com/apparel-fashion-fabric.aspx" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">satin</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, that’s airy but will still keep you warm during cooler nights. Playing with fabric means you can also play with styling; experiment by dressing it up for a night out or down for a casual yet still chic look.  </span></p>
<p><b>Color</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> -- While the original bomber jacket was made in classic </span><a href="http://www.whowhatwear.com/tags/military-green-jacket/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">military green</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, there now exists a vast array of colors to select from based on your personal style. Pair darker, sultry colors with date-night outfits. You can even go for a bold color to create an instant statement outfit regardless of pairing it with casual jeans or with a skirt.</span></p>
<p><b>Fit</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> -- Womenswear bomber jackets typically have a more tailored fit. This more </span><a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2008/01/31/it-look-flawless-tailorin_n_84234.html" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">feminine silhouette</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> will hug your body to give you a slimmer looks.  For more coverage, however, opt for a longer bomber jacket that hits beyond your hips. The added length will keep you warmer, which is an added bonus if your hometown weather tends to </span><a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/news/2016/02/13/polar-vortex-drives-bitter-cold-central-east/80335234/" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">dip into arctic temperatures</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
