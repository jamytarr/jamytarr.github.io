<p><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fashion_illustration" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Fashion illustration</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> was almost thought to be a lost art form. The steady rise of </span><a href="http://www.dpreview.com/?utm_campaign=internal-link&amp;utm_source=logo&amp;utm_medium=image&amp;ref=logo" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">digital photography</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> was beginning to take fashion illustrators’ place in major fashion houses and magazines.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">This skillful ability to wield a pen or brush in such a way that transforms inanimate garments and accessories into animate products was declining -- until now.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Illustrators are now coming out of the shadows and being recognized for their talent by the masses all thanks to social media. The accession of </span><a href="https://www.instagram.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Instagram</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="https://www.pinterest.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pinterest</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> has allowed modern day illustrators to showcase their work through digital curation. Their ability to exhibit their work in real time gives these talented yet undiscovered fashion illustrators a voice.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The buzz about the new-age fashion illustrator crowd was noticed by major players, such as the </span><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/11/fashion/seeing-fashion-with-a-sketchpad-and-markers.html?_r=0" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">New York Times</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.vanityfair.com/style/2015/09/fashion-illustrators-instagram-paperfashion-meagan-morrison-alex-kim" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Vanity Fair</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.vogue.com/13331120/fashion-illustrators-instagram-jenny-walton-carly-kuhn/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Vogue</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="http://nymag.com/thecut/2013/10/first-look-90-contemporary-fashion-illustrators.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The Cut</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> along with other blogs like </span><a href="http://www.manrepeller.com/2015/08/fashion-illustrators-on-instagram-as-the-new-bloggers.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Man Repeller</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, just to name a few.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">With so many venues for sharing their illustrations, these artists are being recognized by brands the way that </span><a href="http://fashionbloggersconnect.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><span style="font-weight: 400;">fashion bloggers</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> are.</span></p>
