<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For the April 2016 cover, </span><a href="http://www.wmagazine.com/" target="_blank" title="W Magazine homepage"><span style="font-weight: 400;">W Magazine</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> cast teenage Hollywood stars dressed in the latest designer duds from </span><a href="http://www.vogue.com/fashion-shows/spring-2016-couture/christian-dior" target="_blank" title="Dior spring summer 2016"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dior</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.vogue.com/fashion-shows/spring-2016-ready-to-wear/givenchy" target="_blank" title="Givenchy spring summer 2016"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Givenchy</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.vogue.com/fashion-shows/spring-2016-ready-to-wear/valentino" target="_blank" title="Valentino spring summer 2016"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Valentino</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="http://www.vogue.com/fashion-shows/spring-2016-ready-to-wear/versace" target="_blank" title="Versace spring summer 2016"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Versace</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. This particular band of starlets, however, is different than the typical glossy supermodel cover.  </span></p>
<p><a href="http://www.dazeddigital.com/artsandculture/article/29598/1/kiernan-shipka-wide-awake-in-dreamland" target="_blank" title="Kiernan Shipka about"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kiernan Shipka</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/samstryker/zendaya-had-the-perfect-response-to-body-shaming" target="_blank" title="Zendaya about"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Zendaya</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="http://fashionista.com/2016/03/willow-smith-chanel" target="_blank" title="Willow Smith Chanel ad"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Willow Smith</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> are all creating awareness around vital social issues. They’re using their fame and large social media following to take a stand on topics like race, gender fluidity, bullying and feminism.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The message that these “Dream Teens” are individually advocating for is: be yourself.</span></p>
