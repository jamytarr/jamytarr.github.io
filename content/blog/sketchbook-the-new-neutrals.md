<p>I've been looking for ways to refresh my wardrobe and perspective on style for 2021 and have been inspired by the idea of the <span style="text-decoration: underline;">New Neutral</span>. </p>
<p> When you think of the typical neutral, you think of the colors below:</p>
<p><img src="/shopify-assets/cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0182/5617/files/Screen_Shot_2021-01-25_at_2.41.30_PM_600x600-33c296e9.png" width="600x600" height="600x600" style="float: none;"></p>
<meta charset="utf-8">
<p><em>(above neutral palette source www.theconceptwardrobe.com)</em></p>
<p>But a New Neutral is a color that is special. It isn't a pastel, but rather a dusty or earthy sister version of a pastel. It looks more like these:</p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><img src="/shopify-assets/cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0182/5617/files/Screen_Shot_2021-01-25_at_2.50.35_PM_600x600-9a00afee.png" alt="" style="float: none;"></div>
<div style="text-align: center;"></div>
<p><em>(New Neutral color palette above source @cypress.and.wool instagram) </em></p>
<p>What I especially like about New Neutrals, is that they work really well with your more basic neutral palette and uplift the entire look. Check out your wardrobe to see if you have any new neutrals and give them a try with a Navy or Army green....</p>
